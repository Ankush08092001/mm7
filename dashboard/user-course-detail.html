What is the main difference between the Otto cycle and the Diesel cycle?

The main difference lies in the ignition method. In the Otto cycle (used in gasoline engines), ignition is caused by a spark plug. In the Diesel cycle, ignition occurs due to the high temperature generated by compressing the air in the cylinder (compression ignition).

Asked by Priya Singh | Answered by Capt. James Davis | 2 days ago

Why are 2-stroke engines commonly used for large container ships?

Large, slow-speed 2-stroke engines are preferred for large vessels due to their high thermal efficiency, ability to burn cheaper heavy fuel oil (HFO), simpler construction (fewer moving parts like valves), and high power output at low rotational speeds, which is ideal for direct propeller drive.
